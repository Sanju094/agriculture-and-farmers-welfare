Coarse Cereals / Millets

Coarse Cereals and Millets are the short duration warm weather (Kharif) crops used both as food and fodder.
Important millets are Jawar, Bajra, Ragi etc.
The areas under these crops have fallen drastically in recent years in India. The coarse cereals and millets are grown
in areas with high temperature and are called dryland crops because can be grown in areas with 50-100 cm rainfall.
The coarse cereal crops are less sensitive to soil deficiencies.
They can be grown in inferior alluvial or loamy soil.
Top three states with maximum production of total coarse cereals are Maharashtra, Karnataka, and Rajasthan.
